This is a running list of my favorite papers in Economics and adjacent fields.

- Fujiwara, Thomas. 2015. ["Voting Technology, Political Responsiveness, and Infant Health: Evidence from Brazil."](https://doi.org/10.3982/ECTA11520) *Econometrica* 83 (2): 423–464.
- Hayek, Friedrich A. 1945. ["The Use of Knowledge in Society."](https://www.jstor.org/stable/1809376) *American Economic Review* 35 (4): 519–530.
- Stigler, George J., and Gary S. Becker. 1977. ["De Gustibus Non Est Disputandum."](https://www.jstor.org/stable/1807222) *American Economic Review* 67 (2): 76–90.
- Thaler, Richard. 1980. ["Toward a Positive Theory of Consumer Choice."](https://doi.org/10.1016/0167-2681(80)90051-7) *Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization* 1 (1): 39–60.
- Kőszegi, Botond, and Matthew Rabin. 2006. ["A Model of Reference-Dependent Preferences."](https://doi.org/10.1093/qje/121.4.1133) *Quarterly Journal of Economics* 121 (4): 1133–1165.
- Bursztyn, Leonardo, Alessandra L. González, and David Yanagizawa-Drott. 2020. ["Misperceived Social Norms: Women Working Outside the Home in Saudi Arabia."](https://doi.org/10.1257/aer.20180975) *American Economic Review* 110 (10): 2997–3029.
- Eyster, Erik, and Matthew Rabin. 2005. ["Cursed Equilibrium."](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1468-0262.2005.00631.x) *Econometrica* 73 (5): 1623–1672.
- O’Donoghue, Ted, and Matthew Rabin. 1999. [“Doing It Now or Later.”](https://doi.org/10.1257/aer.89.1.103) *American Economic Review* 89 (1): 103–124.
- Acemoglu, D., and J. A. Robinson. 2000. [“Why Did the West Extend the Franchise? Democracy, Inequality, and Growth in Historical Perspective.”](https://doi.org/10.1162/003355300555042) *The Quarterly Journal of Economics 115 (4): 1167–99.*
